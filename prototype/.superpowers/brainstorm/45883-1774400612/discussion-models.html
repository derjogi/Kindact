Discussion Model: Kialo-style vs Reddit-style vs Hybrid

Which structure best serves Kindact's goal of surfacing the best ideas and making concerns visible?

THESIS
"Install bioswale + larger pipes on Elm St"
⬆ PRO
"Bioswale is cheaper long-term"
↳ CON
"Requires ongoing maintenance"
"Permeable pavement reduces runoff 30%"
⬇ CON
"Cost estimate seems too low"
"Traffic disruption during construction"

A — Kialo-style Argument Tree

Every contribution is a claim in a strict pro/con tree. No freeform chat. Forces structured thinking. You navigate by drilling into branches.

Pros

  • Forces clarity — every post must be a clear claim
  • Easy to see which arguments have strong/weak rebuttals
  • Great for complex policy debates

Cons

  • Rigid — can't ask questions, share experiences, or have natural dialogue
  • High barrier to contribute (must frame everything as a claim)
  • Loses the social/community feel

B — Reddit-style Threaded Discussion

Traditional comment threads with nested replies, upvotes/downvotes. Natural conversation flow. What you already have.

Pros

  • Familiar — low barrier to participate
  • Natural dialogue: questions, experiences, humor all welcome
  • Good for community building

Cons

  • Popular ≠ best — upvotes reward agreement, not quality
  • Hard to see overall argument structure
  • Threads go off-topic; key points get buried

C — Hybrid: Spotlight + Threads + AI

Reddit-style threads as the base, but with spotlighted comments (semi-random selection from top/new/random), AI search ("is anyone talking about X?"), and AI-generated summary that identifies argument clusters.

Pros

  • Low barrier (threaded discussion) + structured visibility (spotlights)
  • AI search solves "is this already discussed?" problem
  • Semi-random spotlights surface overlooked perspectives
  • Can optionally tag comments as pro/con for structured view

Cons

  • More complex to build
  • AI summaries could be wrong or biased
  • Spotlight algorithm needs careful tuning

💡 Note

These aren't mutually exclusive — Option C could include a "structured view" toggle that shows a Kialo-like argument tree derived from pro/con-tagged comments. But the primary interaction model matters for the data structure and mock data we're about to create.
