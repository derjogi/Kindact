[Solution space & how it solves/helps things]

- Better Decision Making
    - Basis for everything moving forward; helps answering:
        - What should & could we do?
            - Should: What do we *want*, and what of that is ‘omni-positive’ (positive for every stakeholder, i.e. avoiding downsides as much as we can foresee)
            - Could: Subset of should, depends a lot on what resources, labor and knowledge is and can be made available.
        - How can we overcome existing limitations & hurdles?
            - E.g. how can we go against extractive capitalist tendencies? …
            - A solid discussion & decision making platform can help think through possible solutions
    - Involving whoever needs to be involved, not limited to only politicians/those who have the means/…
        - Wisdom of Crowds is good for finding ‘averages’, e.g. numerical estimates, but not so much for other nuanced topics
        - Citizen Assemblies (CA’s) are better for social decisions, but take more time & effort
        - Liquid/Delegated voting with categories - If I don’t want to be involved I can delegate my voice to someone else I trust. This creates a beautiful mix of representative and direct democracy!
        - How can we make bottom-up & top-down interact better? (propagate decisions from CA’s up and make them binding? Do we even *need* a ‘top’ (i.e. politicians) if we have a really good CA system?)
        - Meritocracy, but not based on traditional capitalist measures (i.e. the rich made it → have merit); but instead on peer-to-peer delegation & endorsements
        - 
- Better incentives
    - Fight against extractive & pollutive capitalist (molochian) tendencies:
        - Find ways to value natural assets (currently only a cut down tree has market value)
        - Find ways to value the commons (air is plentiful and ‘free’ → we don’t ‘value’ it → pollute it)
        - Find ways to value unseen work (e.g. Parents raising children)
        - → Find ways to incentivize what we (any group of people at any scale) actually value, outside of the narrow perspective of money
        - Find ways to let different value systems co-exist harmoniously
            - Different religions, different cultures, etc have different values and priorities, with their own justification & acceptance (no absolute truth)
            - Different regions have different constraints that influnece what they value; e.g.
                - lot of water but little flat land → Land & property rights etc are more important
                - much land but it’s dry → Value water more → Harsher laws to protect water.
            - Different beliefs in scientific/cultural advances; e.g. climate change (or not) 
            → one region might introduce speed limits, the other doesn’t. (How do they coexist / negotiate how to drive? How can this be communicated to constituents?)
- Continuous fair redistribution of power (aka money)
    - Currently: Rich become richer through capital giving dividends etc (easier profitable investments); extracted from the rest → Widening gap
    - New: Power from the people, by giving them indirect access to printing money (within limits, of course)
        - E.g. Digital tradeable currencies that gets **created** when *valued tasks (see above)* are implemented; giving power directly into the hands of those that create and execute these tasks (everyone, see Better Decision making above)
        - Mechanisms to prevent abuse
        - Mechanisms to control currency valuation (taxation, inflation, demurrage, …)
- Collaboration & competition
    - Competition is not bad, as long as it doesn’t lead to winner-takes-all dynamics that create feedback loops
    - In the end we all have to collaborate though towards achieving and supporting each other so we can achieve the goals (SDGs, …)
        - ‘Friendly’ competition → better product/approach gets more, but other approaches are also valuable and can get money even if they’re not eventually used - even the attempt to explore something new is worthwhile (see also ‘Do what we value’ above)
